# Sanitized Social Rules Example

This file shows how a fictional company might explain its social triage policy in plain English.

Company: `Northstar Learning Labs`

Executive: `Founder`

Delegates:

- Support Lead: `support@example.com`
- Operations Lead: `ops@example.com`
- Partnership Lead: `partnerships@example.com`
- Finance Lead: `finance@example.com`
- Community Manager: `community@example.com`

## Operating Principle

The social triage agent should eliminate noise by default and surface only:

- Real decisions
- Known relationships
- Customer risk
- Legal or reputation risk
- High-value opportunities
- Items a delegate should handle

## Example Rules

### Spam And Flirt Bait

Ignore, delete, or reject. Do not show the founder.

### Support Issues

Send to the Support Lead unless the customer is extremely upset, public, high-value, or legally threatening.

### Partnership Requests

Send routine partnership requests to the Partnership Lead. Create a decision item only for high-value opportunities.

### Commission Or Payout Issues

Send to the Finance Lead with the sender, source link, exact ask, and context.

### Known Contacts

Qualify what they need. Create a decision item when the relationship matters.

### 911

Create a decision item and send an urgent notification when:

- Legal risk is present
- A customer is extremely angry
- A public reputation issue is forming
- A personal reach-out seems important

## What Not To Include In A Public Repo

- Real customer examples
- Real employee names
- Real phone numbers
- Real group URLs
- Real social handles
- OAuth tokens
- Browser sessions
- Private message screenshots
